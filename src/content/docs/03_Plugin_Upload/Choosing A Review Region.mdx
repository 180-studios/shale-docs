A **review region** defines the area of your scene that Shale will capture. It determines where capture nodes are placed — each node is a position a reviewer can jump to and look around from.

## Grid vs Spline

Shale offers two tools for defining a capture region:

- **Grid** — places capture nodes in a 3D volumetric cage. Good for covering a defined area or room uniformly, where reviewers should be able to stand anywhere within the space.
- **Spline** — places capture nodes along a path. Good for corridors, walkways, or any space where you want to guide the reviewer along a specific route.

Both tools work in full 3D — X, Y, and Z — so they can cover multi-level spaces and environments with vertical complexity.

Go to the **Capture** tab in the Shale panel and click **Add Grid** or **Add Spline** to place a region in your scene.

See the [Grid Tool](./grid-tool) and [Spline Tool](./spline-tool) pages for detailed settings.
